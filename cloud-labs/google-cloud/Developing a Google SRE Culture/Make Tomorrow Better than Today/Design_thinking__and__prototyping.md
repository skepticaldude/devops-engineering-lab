# Design Thinking and Prototyping in SRE

When organizations reduce the **cost of failure** by implementing gradual change practices such as **CI/CD** and **canary deployments**, teams gain the freedom to innovate.

Knowing that every change will be tested allows individuals to **think bigger** and avoid restricting their creativity or ideas.

This is why **design thinking** and **prototyping** are important parts of **Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) organizational culture**.

---

# What is Design Thinking?

**Design thinking** is an approach that combines **creativity and structure** to solve complex problems.

Companies like **Google** use design thinking to teach teams and individuals how to think creatively. This mindset is a key step in the **innovation process**.

The design thinking methodology consists of **five phases**.

---

# The Five Phases of Design Thinking

## 1. Empathize

Observe and engage with your intended users to understand them better and immerse yourself in their environment.

Empathy helps you set aside your own assumptions so you can gain insight into your users and their real needs.

---

## 2. Define the Problem

Clearly define the problem you want to solve.

Instead of focusing on what *you* want to accomplish, express the problem from the **user’s point of view**.

---

## 3. Ideate

Once the problem is defined, start **generating possible solutions**.

This is the stage where you should **think outside the box** and explore as many ideas as possible.

---

## 4. Prototype

Turn ideas into something tangible.

Prototyping allows you to bring ideas **out of your head and into the real world**.  
It is meant to be **experimental**, helping you identify the best possible solution before fully committing.

---

## 5. Test

Test the prototype with **real users in real-world scenarios**.

This allows teams to gather feedback and refine solutions.

---

# A Simpler Software Development Perspective

From a software development mindset, the process can be simplified to:

1. **Focus on the user**
2. **Apply 10x thinking** (think much bigger than incremental improvements)
3. **Prototype the solution**
4. **Test gradually using SRE practices**

Gradual testing can include:

- **CI/CD pipelines**
- **Canary deployments**

These practices allow teams to validate changes safely.

---

# Why Prototyping Matters in SRE Culture

Organizations with SRE teams should promote a **prototype-driven culture**.

Without prototyping:

- Fewer ideas are tested
- Failures occur **slowly**
- Innovation decreases

With a prototyping culture:

- Teams test **more ideas**
- Failures happen **faster**
- Learning happens sooner
- Success becomes more likely

---

# Ways to Prototype Quickly

There is **no single correct way to prototype**. Teams can experiment with many approaches.

### Physical Prototyping
Build simple models using **LEGO blocks or other building materials**.

### Paper and Drawing
Sketch ideas using **pencil and paper** or simple wireframes.

### Clickable Prototypes
Create **interactive mockups** that simulate a solution.

### Role Play
Have team members **act as users or testers** to simulate real scenarios.

### Video Prototypes
Record a video showing how a solution works and observe how users respond.

---

# Tips for Effective Prototyping

No matter the method, prototypes should be **real enough for users to interact with**.

More concrete prototypes lead to:

- Better feedback
- Clearer insights
- More actionable improvements

---

# Real-World Example

Google has observed that **simple prototypes** help customers improve even very complex processes.

Prototypes stimulate **imaginative thinking** and motivate individuals to suggest **bold and ambitious ideas**.

Examples of prototypes created by teams include:

- Video panel discussions
- Heat maps
- Banners with posted notes

---

# Case Study: Retailer in the Netherlands

One leading online retailer in the Netherlands used **design thinking** to improve their production process.

Their team created a simple prototype using **paper cups**:

- Each cup represented a **step in the process**
- Different colored cups marked steps that needed to be:
  - Improved
  - Deprecated

This simple visualization helped the team **identify inefficiencies and brainstorm improvements**.

---

# Key Takeaway

A culture of **design thinking and prototyping** helps organizations:

- Encourage creativity
- Test ideas faster
- Fail quickly and learn
- Innovate more effectively

Supporting this culture is essential for teams working within **SRE environments**.
