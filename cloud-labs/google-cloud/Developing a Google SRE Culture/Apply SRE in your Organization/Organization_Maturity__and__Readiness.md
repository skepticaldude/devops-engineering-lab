# Assessing Organizational Maturity for SRE Adoption

Before implementing SRE principles, it is **important to assess your organization’s maturity** for adopting SRE practices.

---

## The Three-Part SRE Journey

- **SLOs with consequences**  
- **Make tomorrow better than today**  
- **Regulate workload**

---

## Organizational Maturity Levels

- **Low Maturity**:  
  The organization has **not yet adopted SRE principles, practices, and culture**.

- **High Maturity**:  
  The organization has a **well-established SRE team**, or SRE principles, practices, and culture are widely **understood, implemented, and embraced**.

---

## Indicators of High SRE Maturity

1. **Well-documented, user-centric SLOs**  
   - Target reliability correlated with customer happiness.

2. **Error Budgets**  
   - Defined as the difference between perfection and your SLO.  
   - Allow IT teams to move fast while ensuring reliability actions if the service fails.

3. **Blameless Postmortem Culture**  
   - Recognizes that failures are **system problems**, not individual faults.

4. **Low Tolerance for Toil**  
   - Toil: manual, repetitive, automatable, tactical work without enduring value, scaling linearly with service growth.

> These principles can be adopted by any team responsible for production systems, even **before staffing an SRE team**.

---

## Recommended Google Steps for SRE Adoption

1. **Set up SLOs and Error Budgets**
2. **Establish a Blameless Postmortem Culture**
3. **Define and Organize SRE Teams**
4. **Implement Automation and Regulate Workload**

> The **first step may vary** depending on your organizational culture and business needs.
