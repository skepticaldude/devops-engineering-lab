# SRE Culture: Goal Setting, Transparency, and Data-Driven Decision Making

An important **SRE practice** is to measure everything, including **reliability** and **toil**.  
To do this effectively, organizations need a **culture of goal setting, transparency, and data-driven decision making**.

---

## Goal Setting

To implement goal setting in SRE:

1. Create a **data-driven goal setting process**.
2. Identify **KPIs** for what and who you are measuring.
3. Decide **what to measure and how**.

Google uses **OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)** as KPIs:

- OKRs are graded on a scale of **0.0 to 1.0**, with 1.0 meaning fully achieved.
- The **optimal grade** for OKRs is **60–70%**.
- OKRs reflect **contributions and impact**, not performance evaluations.
- Organizational OKRs are **graded publicly**, promoting transparency.
- **Frequent check-ins** help teams and individuals maintain progress.
- OKRs encourage teams to **think big**.

---

## Embracing Transparency

Transparency demonstrates trust in employees and helps them perform better.

Key practices include:

- **Sharing tools**:  
  - Example: Google’s issue tracker **Buganizer** allows all teams to see issues and resolutions.
- **Sharing communications and feedback loops**:  
  - Feedback loops involve producing something, measuring outcomes, and using that data to improve.
- **Leadership involvement**:  
  - Apply the **head, heart, feet model** to guide teams through change effectively.

---

## Data-Driven Decision Making

To make **objective decisions**, organizations must **remove unconscious biases**.

### Common Biases:

- **Affinity bias**: Favoring people similar to yourself (race, gender, education, etc.)
- **Confirmation bias**: Seeking information that supports pre-existing notions
- **Labeling bias**: Judging people by external appearance
- **Selective attention bias**: Focusing on input from preferred individuals

These biases can impact:

- Diversity and inclusivity
- Creativity and innovation
- Team morale and engagement
- Employee retention

---

### Removing Unconscious Bias

1. **Question first impressions**: Avoid relying solely on initial judgments, especially in hiring or promotions.
2. **Justify decisions**: Document reasoning to stay accountable and reduce bias.
3. **Decide collectively**: Encourage team discussion and feedback to check biases.
4. **Call out bias**: Speak up when you notice unconscious bias; mistakes are okay and are learning opportunities.

---

## Outcome

By fostering a culture of:

- **Goal setting**
- **Transparency**
- **Data-driven decision making**
- **Bias awareness and mitigation**

Teams can **make objective, informed decisions**, identify areas for improvement, and empower individuals to speak up when bias affects outcomes.
