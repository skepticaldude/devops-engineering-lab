# Skills for Site Reliability Engineers (SREs)

In addition to understanding the **practical and cultural fundamentals**, SREs require several skills to support an organization’s adoption of Site Reliability Engineering.

---

## Who to Hire

- An **SRE is an engineer who also runs operations**.  
- Unless hiring an experienced SRE, **current or new engineers may need upskilling**.  
- At Google, SREs are often **systems administrators or operations engineers** with some scripting experience, who receive **software engineering training** to improve their engineering skills.

> Training and resources for new SREs is an investment that benefits the organization in the long term.

---

## Key Skills to Train and Hire For

### 1. Operations and Software Engineering
- Experience running applications and production gives **critical operational insights**.  
- Engineers without operations experience will need upskilling.  
- Operators (e.g., sysadmins) need to **understand the software** they support and be empowered to improve it.

### 2. Monitoring Systems
- Understanding monitoring systems is necessary to **measure and account for SLOs**.

### 3. Production Automation
- Scaling operations requires **automation skills** for processes and tasks.

### 4. System Architecture
- SREs should understand **system architecture** and how to scale applications effectively.

### 5. Troubleshooting
- Being on-call regularly requires **analytical and inquisitive troubleshooting skills**.

### 6. Culture of Trust
- SREs share ownership of services with developers.  
- Key characteristics of a healthy culture: **communication, agreement, trust**.  
- Extend this culture to other teams, such as **security and privacy**.

### 7. Incident Management
- Combines **technical troubleshooting** and **communication frameworks**.  
- Skills needed:  
  - Timely problem resolution  
  - Clear and explicit communication  
  - Task and time management  
  - Record keeping

---

## Important Character Traits

- **Resilience** – Handle ambiguity between feature velocity and reliability goals.  
- **Flexibility** – Balance enabling product development with protecting customer experience.

> Starting with these skills, along with implementing SLOs, error budgets, and postmortems, prepares your organization to form its first SRE team.
